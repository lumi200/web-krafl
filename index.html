How fonts feel and pair

While fonts can be categorized into specific, applying them strictly might not always give a great result. Many typefaces blur the line between those categories. Rather than follow the categories and their use cases, you should look at a font separately and experiment what fits best for your project. Still, the categories and what feeling they convey might be helpful to start

Sans Serif

Sans Serif looks modern, looks simple, minimal and clean. Usually, all strokes of the Font are the same width. Sans-Serif fonts are the most used category when in comes to the web. They are fine if you need them for long text bodies, for a user interface or as Headings or as Headings

Serif

Serif typefaces have tapers on them and have been used since the 15th century in printing. These fonts give a warm, traditional and trustworthy feeling. They are good for headings and long text bodies, but shouldn't be used in an interface.

Slab Serif

The Slab Serif fonts are less smooth and have more hard edges. They were invented in the 19th Century and look "wild western". They give of a stable, confident and clunky feeling. Same as the Serif fonts, they can be used for text bodies and headings, but shouldn't be used in an interface

Script

Script fonts are the opposite of simple and clean, they are inspired by calligraphy typefaces. The category for this typeface is very broad and includes many styles. This typeface should exclusively be used for headings. Because else, readability suffers a lot.

Handwritten

While script fonts try to be fancy, handwritten fonts try to be relatable and "normal" and give of a human, friendly and authentic feeling. Same as the script fonts, this should only be used as a heading. While not as difficult to read as most script fonts, it's still harder compared to other fonts

Display

Display fonts, to simplify, is everything that doesn't fit in a category above. These typefaces can be very atmospheric. Since they can look very different, they don't give of any specific feeling, rather they strike in their unique. Still, they should only be used in a heading.
